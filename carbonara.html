Descripcion

Carbonara is a Roman dish made with eggs, hard cheese, and cured pork. Its signature rich and silky sauce comes from beaten eggs tossed with hot pasta. The trick to making carbonara is making sure the pasta is hot enough to cook the eggs, but not so hot that they curdle. Buon appetito!

Ingredients

  • Olive oil
  • Guanciale or cured pork cheek
  • Spaghetti
  • Eggs
  • Pecorino Romano Cheese
  • Salt
  • Pepper

step by step

  1. Cook the pork in olive oil until browned and crispy, then drain on paper towels.

  2. Boil the spaghetti in salted water. Drain and return to the pot. Let cool.

  3. Whisk the eggs, 1/2 of the cheese, and some pepper in a bowl until smooth.

  4. Pour the egg mixture over the pasta, stirring quickly, until creamy.

  5. Stir in the pork, then top with the remaining cheese and more black pepper.

  6. ENJOY!
